The “Endless Frontier” of Spending and Inflation

What happened:

Congress was considering spending $120 billion on a bill called the Endless Frontier Act.

It would pump federal funding into research areas such as artificial intelligence, robotics, natural disaster prevention or mitigation, biotechnology, distributed ledger technologies, cybersecurity, and so forth.

But these days, how can anyone get excited about a measly $120 billion?

So the bill has been replaced by the United States Innovation and Competition Act of 2021, a 1,445 page bill which incorporates the Endless Frontier Act, and others.

The bill still invests $120 billion in education, science, and technology.

But now, in addition, it hands over $50 billion to the semiconductor industry, at least $37.7 billion to foreign countries, and $10 billion for a lunar landing program seen as a “Bezos bailout”.

(The Amazon founder’s Blue Origin space company recently lost a bid to Elon Musk’s SpaceX to capture a NASA contract for lunar landers, so this is another shot for Bezos to grab the money.)

The bill’s new total comes in north of $200 billion.

What this means:

Senator Everett Dirksen is rumored to have once quipped, “A billion here, a billion there, and pretty soon you're talking about real money.”...
