#066 - Dr. Mark Mattson (listen today!)
 
Released today: Dr. Mark Mattson is one of the most cited neuroscientists in the world, with more than 180,000 citations of his work noted in the scientific literature.
 
His rigorous work has advanced scientific understanding of brain aging, intermittent fasting, and theory on the evolutionary origins of the benefits we derived from stress.
 
 In this excellent, 2.5-hour conversation, Dr. Mattson and I talk a lot about:
  • Time-restricted eating and intermittent fasting (including specifically how it affects older adults vs young, and women vs. men),
  • The benefit of the occasional metabolic switch as a result of fasting-induced ketosis,
  • The ketogenic diet’s effects on brain health, cortisol, resveratrol and spermidine,
  • How plant-based bioactive compounds induce hormetic stress,
  • ...and more!

This episode is available today!
 
Listen on Apple Podcasts or Spotify, or in your favorite podcast player.
 
#067 - Dr. Ashley Mason (listen next week)
 
Dr. Ashley Mason is director of the Sleep, Eating, and Affect Laboratory at the Osher Center for Integrative Medicine at UCSF. She specializes in various different non-pharmacological treatments for changing behaviors including sleep and eating behavior disorders, or affect/mood disorders.

In this in-depth discussion we talk about:
  • Non-pharmacological depression treatment via sauna use
  • Cognitive behavioural therapy for insomnia
  • Effective strategies to change bad behaviours despite known negative health outcomes (including environmental approaches and reinforcement models
  • ...and more!
 
#068 - Dr. Bill Harris - (listen the week of August 30th)
 
If you enjoyed our recent video on omega-3s, you’ll really love this episode: Dr. Bill Harris is one of the world’s foremost experts on omega-3 fatty acids.
 
In 2004 he and his co-author Dr. Von Schacky first proposed that their test, an index of the membrane content of omega-3 found in red blood cells, could advantageously be used to track as a risk factor for heart disease. The index contrasts with other tests, which look at plasma content but are less correlated to long-term omega-3 status.

In this deep dive episode, Dr. Harris and I discuss:
  • Omega-3s ALA, EPA and DHA
  • The Omega-3 index
  • How Omega-3 levels predict all-cause mortality, cardiovascular benefits of Omega-3s, dosage and safety
  • A pilot study looking into Omega-3s and COVID-19
  • ....and more!
