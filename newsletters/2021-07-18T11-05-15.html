Too Many People Support Permanent COVID Restrictions

What happened:

A recent poll asked Britons if they think certain COVID restrictions should:

  1. A) stay in place until some obscure date when “COVID-19 is under control worldwide,” whatever that means, or;
  2. B) remain in place “permanently, regardless of the risk from COVID-19.”

40% of respondents said people should have to wear masks in shops and public transport, forever.

35% believe the ten day quarantine after returning from abroad should remain permanent (which sounds xenophobic).

26% believe all nightclubs and casinos should be permanently closed. Post-pandemic those businesses just shouldn’t exist, apparently.

43% (which includes the 26% in the more extreme category) believe nightclubs and casinos should remain closed until the elusive end of the pandemic, and the government voluntarily gives up all its new powers...

19% believe there should be a permanent 10pm curfew, unless you have a “good reason” to leave home. 27% want to hand that power to the government until “COVID-19 is under control worldwide.”

However, the sample size of the survey was small, with just over 1,000 respondents (although the data was “weighted to match the profile of the population”).

Still, people who think like this are out there, and other polls indicate that these authoritarian views aren’t confined to the UK.

For example, it wasn’t until May that more Americans believed healthy people should “lead their normal lives as much as possible to avoid interruptions to work and business.”

Until late March 2021, over 60% of Americans still believed that healthy people should isolate themselves from friends and family, and barely leave the home for sun or exercise, because of COVID-19.

Another recent poll of Americans showed a huge partisan divide on whether draconian COVID-19 restrictions were necessary.

Democrats were mostly united in supporting any restriction local and state governments thought up. Only 8% said that stay at home orders wrongly curtailed freedom.

Republicans were divided — 42% said that stay at home orders took away too much freedom, and 42% said they were necessary. Among Independents, 21% said lockdowns went too far.

If this poll can be believed, the majority of Americans think the government should be able to place entire populations under house arrest, whenever there is an “emergency” or threat to public health.

The survey also asked if high school students — extremely unlikely to come down with a serious case of COVID-19 — and faculty should have vaccinations forced on them before returning to school. 83% of Democrats, 50% of Independents, and 45% of Republicans said they should.

What this means:

Of course, many people gladly tried to force their views on others even before the pandemic.

But COVID-19 gave tiny tyrants everywhere the excuse they needed to control their neighbors.

Clearly, some Britons have no use for dance clubs and are in bed by 10pm every night. An empathetic person would say, hey different strokes for different folks.

But control freaks would rather turn society into a prison, because they view freedom as inherently dangerous, and think people need to be controlled.

However, until the glorious artificial intelligence gods run our lives, we are stuck with other flawed, human beings controlling us.

And as J.R.R. Tolkien pointed out, “the most improper job of any man, even saints (who at any rate were at least unwilling to take it on), is bossing other men. Not one in a million is fit for it, and least of all those who seek the opportunity.”

What you can do about it:

You can still exercise personal freedom and corresponding responsibility.

One of the best ways to do so is to make a deliberate decision about where you live.

Why would you want to live among people who hate your freedom, and in a democracy, have control over that freedom?

This doesn’t necessarily mean you have to move across the world.

Those surveys of a few thousand people allegedly represent the views of the entire United States — all 330 million people, and 3.5 million square miles.

Obviously that’s not true. There are plenty of places filled with wonderful people who are a joy to live around.

That was a conversation we started last week, as we delved into some ways to explore which area of the your country is best for you.

Remember, much of the COVID tyranny came from local health departments, at the town, city, or county level.

Freedom may be just across the county line. Move to an unincorporated area outside of the city, and the restrictions may no longer apply to you.

Buy a little land with some distance from your neighbors, and suddenly you can invite anyone you want to Thanksgiving and Christmas dinner without having the Gestapo at your door.

At this point, that might be your Plan A... and as you know, we are all about a Plan B.

So it makes sense to consider the threshold at which you will no longer be comfortable living where you live.

And at that point, you should already know where you want to go, and how to get there.

That could mean a second passport from Portugal, or a residency in Mexico. Or it could mean owning a vacation home in Florida, or a bug-out cabin in Montana.

Maybe, like Tim Staermose, who writes Sovereign Man’s 4th Pillar investment newsletter, you’re ready to go real exotic and set yourself up in Tanzania, where your money goes further, and life stayed pretty normal throughout the pandemic.
