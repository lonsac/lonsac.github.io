The Two Ways of Writing

There are two ways to write:
​
1) Top: Work slowly, from beginning to end, and make everything perfect along the way.

2) Bottom: Put all your ideas on paper as fast as possible. Then edit your piece over and over again until your writing is clear.

Writing a Great Book

Yesterday, a friend told me about one of her favorite writers.

Her friend wrote a stunning memoir. After its release, she was asked what it takes to write such an outstanding book. The author said: “If you want to write a good book, write about the things you don’t want others to know about you. If you want to write a great book, write about the things you don’t want to know about yourself.”

​

Photo of the Week

We’ve been hosting a documentary filmmaker in Oaxaca for the past few days.
​
As much as anything, she flew down to try the mole at a restaurant called Las Quince Letras. Its owner, Celia Florian, was the narrator for the Oaxaca-focused episode of Street Food: Latin America (watch it on Netflix here).
​
As it turns out, our guest’s friend directed the episode. When we walked into the restaurant, we found Ms. Florian to say hello on behalf of the director.
​
Her eyes lit up with joy and she immediately sat down with us. What started as a casual hello turned into a surprise seven-course tasting menu, with detailed descriptions of each dish provided by the owner herself. For three and a half hours, Ms. Florian told stories about the Oaxacan ingredients and her 25-year quest to make the perfect plate of mole.

Ultimately, our theme for this trip has been “Go to the source.”
​
From textiles to cuisine, we’ve tried to find craftsmen who are highly-qualified and under-appreciated. For all of us, this meal was a highlight of the trip.
