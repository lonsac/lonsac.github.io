Oregon Scraps Reading and Math Requirement to Graduate

What happened:

A new Oregon law suspends the requirements that high school students must show reading, writing and math proficiency in order to graduate.

The law mandates an evaluation of graduation standards, and requires lawmakers to issue new guidelines in 2024.

But in the meantime, “a student may not be required to show proficiency in Essential Learning Skills as a condition of receiving a high school diploma during the 2021-2022 or 2022-2023 school year.”

That means that no proof is required that students have learned how to read, write, and do math. Students in Oregon will be allowed to graduate as long as they simply take the required classes.

And to make the new standards from 2024 forward “equitable and inclusive,” a slew of testing exceptions and alternatives will be given for those who have a hard time passing tests required to graduate.

For example, the legislation says modified diplomas can be given to students who would otherwise not have graduated, but faced hardships such as being a foster child, or the child of a migrant worker.

To be fair, there are some good arguments against standardized testing, since instead of focusing on providing a good education, the incentive for teachers is to teach what is on the test.

But it’s hard to ignore that removing standards to graduate also removes any accountability for the teachers and school districts who are supposed to be educating the students.

There are plenty of homeschooling methods and curriculum to choose from. But if you value freedom for both yourself and your children— which is probably why you’re here— these are some resources to consider:

“Unschooling”

Unschooling is a more hands-off type of homeschooling method, where children learn through experience, and pursue what interests them. The parents’ role is to provide the opportunities and resources to spark their children’s interests, but not necessarily force their children into a particular type of structured learning.

Wild and Free is a group which provides resources, events, books and courses meant to help “unschoolers” teach their children, while allowing them to remain wild and free.

The founder of Wild and Free, Ainsley Arment, also has a book called The Call of the Wild and Free: Reclaiming Wonder in Your Child's Education.

More Traditional Homeschooling Curriculum

The former Congressman Dr. Ron Paul, famous for campaigning to end the Federal Reserve monetary system in favor of gold-backed currency, offers a homeschool curriculum.

The Ron Paul Curriculum is “pro-free enterprise and pro-business” and even includes home business courses, and a finance course. There are lessons for all ages, in various subjects, and the curriculum includes thousands of video lessons as well.

Home Science Tools offers lab kits with all the materials and instructions needed for various hands-on science lessons in chemistry, biology, and physics for grades K-12.

Mammoth Math, Right Start, and Saxon Math are each math programs tailored for homeschoolers and independent learning.

Whatever your area or level of expertise, there are plenty of resources available to aid you in homeschooling your children. That way you can set your own standards for your children’s education, and not rely on strangers who may or may not share your philosophy on life.
